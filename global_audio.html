Samsung Global Audio Project

Counseled Samsung’s business strategy through
analysis of market trends and user experience feedbacks

Type Client-based Team Project | Wise-UX Global Consulting Team
Client Samsung Consumer Electronics
Role UX Consultant/Researcher
Timespan Summer 2014

Overview

In summer 2014, I worked as an UX consultant at Wise-UX Global Consulting Group and worked on the Samsung Global Audio project. Samsung brought us one of their core business problems and wanted us to address and solve the issues from UX perspectives.

Our global team consisted of eight other consultants including Project Manager, Data Scientist, Business Analyst and UX professionals.

Problem Brief

Initial problem that Samsung brought us was:


"... We ultimately want to make an entry into the high-end & low-end audio market. Therefore, through tailored user study as well as future trends analysis, we want to get help in making our best business decision before jumping into the market..."


After, we re-defined the problem more specifically, by asking questions like:


ㆍ How can we define “high-end” and “low-end” audio market?
ㆍ What are the products included in “global audio market”?
ㆍ What are the short-term and long-term business goals?
ㆍ Are there any specific regions to focus?


By digging deeper into questions, we were able to collaborate with the client to clearly define the problem.

My primary roles

1. Counseled partners in strategy meetings through analysis of market trends and user experience feedbacks, highlighting potential product development and untapped demand of audio market in the U.S. and U.K.

2. Developed individualized strategy proposals for Samsung Global Audio through creating up-to-date databases of high-end users, reviewing firm’s marketing process and potential promotion issues in both domestic and global markets.

Approach

We approached the problem by utilizing various framework and methods. Particularly, we spent substantial time and effort in conducting user study.

Process

Due to confidentiality, I cannot reveal materials I created during the project (references available on request). But as a quick summary about the process, I was able to experience both conducting very tailored UX study and business strategy/marketing analysis.

It was a valuable experiences for me to spend appreciable energy in segmenting user types,conducting trend, industry and competitor analysis, screening and recruiting users as well as utilizing diverse business strategy methods in the process.

Outcomes

We successfully concluded the project, and later this project laid the groundwork for Samsung’s M&A (Merger and Acquisition) of Harman International Industries, Inc., one of the biggest audio, electronic, and infotainment systems companies in the world.

Some of the key features from recently-released Harman audio products, such as smart speaker or new premium sound bars, were things we included in our final solutions.

Final Thoughts

Although Samsung has been regarded as one of the most innovative companies in the world, they are having somewhat of a hard time in applying user-centered business strategy. Through this project, I learned how to incorporate both UX process and strategic business analysis into making core business decisions.

As a person who has experienced several business sectors, now I am confident to say that no strategic business decisions occur without UX feedback. By looking at how Samsung is making a huge growth in the audio industry, I also feel obliged to become a UX professional who can provide both insightful and strategic guidance to every problem-solving task.
